How to Play

Points are scored by passing the disc to a teammate in the opposing end zone. Players must not take steps while holding the disc, and interceptions, incomplete passes, and passes out of bounds are turnovers.

Players

In 2012, there were 5.1 million Ultimate players in the United States.[6] Ultimate is played across the world in pickup games and by recreational, school, club, professional, and national teams at various age levels and with open, women's, and mixed divisions.

History

From 1965 Jared Kass and fellow Amherst students evolved a team frisbee game based on concepts from American football, basketball, and soccer. Jared taught this game to high school student Joel Silver during the summer of 1967 at Mount Hermon Prep school summer camp.
